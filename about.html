Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To inspire hope to the most vulnerable communities in Uganda through sustainable development initiatives and compassionate outreach programs.

Our Vision

A Uganda where every vulnerable individual, family, and community has the opportunity to live with dignity, empowerment, and hope for a better tomorrow.

Our Core Values

Honesty & Integrity

We foster strong and lasting relationships by being trustful and authentic in interactions, activities and relationships.

Responsibility

We are mindful that the activities we perform have an impact on the community and our duty is to inspire people living in it.

Compassion

We walk in the shoes of people we serve and those we work with in our organization.

Respect

We treat others with dignity and understanding regardless of their background, beliefs or abilities.

Loyalty & Transparency

We build trust through honest and transparent communication with our organization, community and partners.

Collaboration

We perform as a team and build on each other's strengths both within our organization and with our partners.

"Inspiring Hope"

Our Journey

DaMaC's story is one of growth, compassion, and dedicated service to vulnerable communities across Uganda.

April 2021

The Beginning

DaMaC was founded by Mr. Dan Magandazi during the challenging days of the COVID-19 pandemic. Our first outreach took place on April 21st, 2021, when we distributed COVID-19 relief to over 20 families and provided essential clothing to more than 40 children at Katubwe Gospel Church in Wakiso, Uganda.

July 2023

Continued Growth & Outreach

DaMaC returned to Katubwe Village to host a clothing donation event that benefited over 80 children. With the support of generous donors, the charity provided new and gently used clothing to these children, many of whom had never experienced the joy of receiving new garments from abroad.

2023

Leadership Expansion

Mr. Kiryowa Deo joined as co-founder, strengthening DaMaC's leadership and vision. Together with a dedicated team of volunteers, they continued to build a foundation committed to inspiring hope and bringing positive change to communities in Uganda.

March 2024

Expanded Community Impact

DaMaC organized a community clothing outreach in Katubwe Village, where over 200 residents received clothing donated by generous supporters in Dubai. The event took place at Katubwe Gospel Church, benefiting men, women, and children in the community.

August 2024

Community Engagement Initiatives

DaMaC held a community engagement event in Bbanda town, beginning with a cleanup effort of the Bbanda Town Center in partnership with the Sipimo Foundation. The day continued with a friendly football match between the DaMaC team and Bbanda community residents, and included the donation of footballs to the PAXX Soccer Academy. Later that day, clothing was distributed to over 500 residents of the Bbanda community.

September 18, 2024

Official Registration

DaMaC officially registered and was certified by the Uganda Registration Services Bureau. The organization's name was changed from Dan Hope Foundation (DHF) to Dan Magandazi Charity Organization (DaMaC), reflecting the essence of our mission and commitment to transparency and accountability.

December 2024

Christmas Charity Outreach

For Christmas 2024, DaMaC extended a heartwarming outreach to about 50 newborn babies and their mothers at Wakiso Health 4 in Wakiso District. Newborns received baby bedsheets, offering comfort and warmth, while mothers received packages of basic foodstuffs to support their wellbeing as they cared for their newborns.

Looking Ahead

Vision for 2035

DaMaC aims to establish the DaMaC Vocational Training Center in vulnerable communities of Uganda, addressing critical issues related to unemployment, poverty, and lack of access to quality education. This initiative seeks to empower individuals, particularly youth and women, with skills needed for sustainable employment or entrepreneurship.

"Greetings all, I am deeply grateful and humbled as I reflect on the journey of DaMaC and the incredible milestones we've achieved since establishment. The inspiration to establish a hope foundation came to me during the challenging days of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2021. It was a time when everyone needed hope to live. At that time, my parents, Pastor Dan and Rose Magandazi, supported and encouraged me to reach out to the people in Katubwe village, providing COVID relief and clothing to those in need. That first outreach became the seed that grew into what DaMaC Organization is today.

In 2023, I was blessed to welcome my long-time friend, Mr. Kiryowa Deo, as my co-founder. Together, with the unwavering commitment of our team of volunteers, we have built a foundation dedicated to inspiring hope and bringing positive change to our communities in Uganda.

I want to take this opportunity to recognize and thank our official partners Sipimo Foundation from China and Divine Grace Empowerment Inc (DGE) from the USA for their invaluable support. Your generosity has been instrumental in helping us grow.

On a personal note, I am overjoyed to share that DaMaC officially registered and was certified by the Uganda Registration Services Bureau on September 18th, 2024. I am also incredibly grateful to my friends and colleagues who played a key role during the clothing donation drive in Dubai, your generous efforts have made a tremendous impact to the community we serve. I am proud of the difference we've made together and I give thanks to God for connecting me with such amazing friends and partners who share and support the vision behind this charity.

Again, I want to express my heartfelt appreciation to the dedicated DaMaC team of executive directors and volunteers. Your passion and commitment make everything we do possible and continues to drive our mission forward. I am incredibly proud of how far we've come and the hundreds of lives we've touched. Seeing DaMaC inspiring hope in communities is more fulfilling than I ever dreamed possible. Together, we are building a future where hope and compassion inspire lasting change in Uganda.

Thank you to everyone who has supported this journey. With your continued dedication, I am excited for the future of DaMaC and the opportunity we have to inspire hope together, the best is yet to come."

Our Vision for the Future

Looking ahead to 2035, we are committed to expanding our impact through sustainable initiatives that create lasting change.

DaMaC Vocational Training Center

By 2035, DaMaC aims to establish a comprehensive Vocational Training Center in Uganda's most vulnerable communities to address critical issues of unemployment, poverty, and limited access to quality education.

Our Motto: Skills for Life

Empowering Through Education

The center will equip youth and women with practical skills in various trades, including fashion design, mobile and computer repair, cosmetology, electrical installation, welding, plumbing, and more.

Economic Independence

Through affordable tuition and flexible payment plans, we'll make quality vocational training accessible, leading to employment opportunities and entrepreneurship development within the community.

Community Transformation

Beyond individual benefits, the center will foster collaboration between learners, local businesses, and community stakeholders, creating a hub for innovation and development.

Through this initiative, we aim to reduce unemployment, enhance self-reliance, and contribute to the overall development and well-being of vulnerable communities across Uganda.
